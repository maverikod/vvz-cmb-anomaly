### A. Setup: base field, linear tail and “no mass terms”

- Base phase substrate: a multi-phase field Θ(x, φ, t) ∈ U(1)^3.  
- Derivative-only Lagrangian (no potentials/mass-terms), cf. earlier sections:
  \[
  \mathcal L_\Theta=\frac{f_\phi^2}{2}\,g^{MN}\partial_M\Theta\,\partial_N\Theta
  +\frac{\beta_4}{4}\,\mathcal I_4[\Theta]
  +\frac{\gamma_6}{6}\,\mathcal I_6[\Theta],
  \quad \mathcal I_4\sim(\Delta\Theta)^2,\ \mathcal I_6\sim|\nabla\Theta|^6.
  \]
- Linear tail (far from cores) is governed by the linearized operator around a slowly varying background state:
  \[
  \partial_t^2\,\delta\Theta + \omega^2(-i\nabla)\,\delta\Theta \simeq 0,\quad
  \omega^2(k)=c_\phi^2 k^2 + \alpha_4 k^4 + \alpha_6 k^6 + \dots
  \]
  where coefficients \(\alpha_{2m}\) are functions of the stiffnesses \((\beta_4,\gamma_6,\dots)\). No mass term is allowed.

### B. Big Bang as a global quench (phenomenological postulate)

- “Big Bang” ≡ rapid quench of the medium parameters at \(t=t_q\): the parameter vector
  \(\lambda(t)=\{f_\phi(t),\beta_4(t),\gamma_6(t),\dots\}\) changes on a short time-scale
  \(\tau_q\) (with \(\tau_q\ll \omega_k^{-1}\) for a broad k-band).  
- Before/after quench frequencies:
  \[
  \omega_k^{(-)}=\omega(k;\lambda^-),\qquad \omega_k^{(+)}=\omega(k;\lambda^+).
  \]
- Mode equation in k-space:
  \[
  \ddot{\delta\Theta}_{\boldsymbol k} + \omega_k^2(t)\,\delta\Theta_{\boldsymbol k}=0,\qquad
  \omega_k^2(t)=
  \begin{cases}
    \big(\omega_k^{(-)}\big)^2,& t<t_q\\
    \big(\omega_k^{(+)}\big)^2,& t>t_q
  \end{cases}
  \]
- Sudden-quench excitation (Bogoliubov coefficients). For \(\tau_q\to 0\):
  \[
  |\beta_k|^2=\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{\omega_k^{(+)}}{\omega_k^{(-)}}-\frac{\omega_k^{(-)}}{\omega_k^{(+)}}\right)^2,\qquad
  n_k\equiv |\beta_k|^2.
  \]
  This fixes the post-quench occupation numbers of linear tail modes without invoking any potentials or mass terms.

### C. Tail energy spectrum and algebraic spatial decay

- Spectral energy density right after the quench:
  \[
  \mathcal E_{\rm tail}(k)\;=\;\omega_k^{(+)}\,|\beta_k|^2\,\mathcal N_0,
  \]
  where \(\mathcal N_0\) sets the overall normalization from the background state just before the quench (a single global factor once units are fixed).
- Algebraic far-field in real space (fractional-rigidity imprint). For a broad class of derivative-only operators with fractional rigidity (see FRAC-1), the retarded kernel yields
  \[
  G_\beta(r)\ \propto\ r^{\,2\beta-3}\quad (r\to\infty),
  \]
  hence the late-time equal-time two-point correlation of the tail obeys
  \[
  \xi_\Theta(r)\equiv \langle \delta\Theta(x)\delta\Theta(x+r)\rangle
  \ \sim\ r^{-(3-2\beta)}\,\cos(\kappa_\star r+\varphi_\star),
  \]
  i.e. algebraic “ripples” with preferred spacing \(\Delta r\simeq \pi/\kappa_\star\) and no exponential envelopes.

### D. Effective “background radiation” (artifact of the high-frequency base field)

- Define the observable intensity proxy in the linear tail:
  \[
  I(x)\;=\;\big|\partial_t \delta\Theta(x,t)\big|^2\;+\;c_\phi^2\,\big|\nabla \delta\Theta(x,t)\big|^2,
  \]
  evaluated over a short averaging window compared to observation time scales.
- The effective temperature fluctuation (CMB-like observable) on the sky direction \(\hat{\boldsymbol n}\) is modeled as a line-of-sight projection through the last-coherence layer \(\Sigma_\star\) (phase-FRW language, no real expansion):
  \[
  \frac{\delta T}{T}(\hat{\boldsymbol n})
  \;=\;\int_0^\infty dr\ W(r)\ \int\frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\ e^{i\boldsymbol k\cdot r\hat{\boldsymbol n}}\ \mathcal T(k)\ \delta\Theta_{\boldsymbol k},
  \]
  where \(W(r)\) is a narrow window around \(\Sigma_\star\) and \(\mathcal T(k)\) is a (dimensionless) linear transfer from phase fluctuations to intensity proxy. Both are determined by the medium; no potentials are introduced.

### E. Angular power spectrum

- Expand \(\delta T/T\) in spherical harmonics: \(a_{\ell m}=\int d\Omega\ Y^\ast_{\ell m}\ \delta T/T\).  
  Under statistical isotropy of the tail,
  \[
  C_\ell\equiv \langle |a_{\ell m}|^2\rangle
  \;=\;4\pi\int_0^\infty dk\,k^2\ P_{\Theta}(k)\ \big|\Delta_\ell(k)\big|^2,
  \]
  with
  \[
  P_{\Theta}(k)=\langle |\delta\Theta_{\boldsymbol k}|^2\rangle
  = \frac{\mathcal E_{\rm tail}(k)}{\omega_k^{(+)}}\ \propto\ |\beta_k|^2,
  \qquad
  \Delta_\ell(k)=\int_0^\infty dr\ W(r)\,\mathcal T(k)\,j_\ell(kr).
  \]
- Scaling prediction. If at intermediate k the fractional-rigidity tail dominates with effective index \(\beta\) so that
  \(P_{\Theta}(k)\propto k^{-(3-2\beta)}\) (Fourier pair to \(\xi_\Theta(r)\sim r^{-(3-2\beta)}\)), then for a thin \(\Sigma_\star\) the Limber-like estimate gives
  \[
  C_\ell\ \propto\ \ell^{-(3-2\beta)}\quad (\text{intermediate }\ell),
  \]
  while oscillatory “acoustic-like” ripples in \(C_\ell\) arise from the \(\cos(\kappa_\star r)\) factor via the Bessel projection (spacing \(\Delta\ell\sim \pi\,r_\star\,\kappa_\star\)).

### F. Consistency with phase-FRW and constraints

- Phase-FRW background (no true expansion): the effective background enters only through \(W(r)\) and \(\mathcal T(k)\) built from the eikonal of the medium; global coherence is set by \(c_\phi\gg c\) (no horizon issue for phase).
- Passivity (no negative dissipation): \(\Re\,\Gamma_{\rm mem}\ge 0\); energy in the tail is non-negative and conserved in the linear limit.
- “No mass terms” and “no exponentials”: kernels and spectra are derivative/fractional-based; large-r tails and high-k falloffs are algebraic. Any observed near-thermal shape must emerge as an effective windowed power-law mixture rather than a fundamental exponential potential.

### G. Quench parameters and practical inversion

- Post-quench spectrum from mode-matching:
  \[
  P_{\Theta}(k)\;=\;A_q\ \frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{\omega_k^{(+)}}{\omega_k^{(-)}}-\frac{\omega_k^{(-)}}{\omega_k^{(+)}}\right)^2,\quad
  \omega_k^{(\pm)}=\sqrt{c_\phi^2 k^2 + \alpha_4^{(\pm)}k^4 + \alpha_6^{(\pm)}k^6+\dots},
  \]
  with \(A_q\) a single global amplitude (units). The set \(\{\alpha_{2m}^{(\pm)}\}\) and \(\kappa_\star\) (from the ripple spacing) are the physical knobs of the medium.
- Observable mapping:
  \[
  C_\ell(A_q,\{\alpha_{2m}^{(\pm)}\},\kappa_\star; W,\mathcal T)\;=\;4\pi\!\int dk\,k^2\,P_{\Theta}(k)\,|\Delta_\ell(k)|^2.
  \]
  Fitting \(C_\ell\) and the ripple spacing yields \(\kappa_\star\) and the post-/pre-quench ratios \(\omega_k^{(+)}/\omega_k^{(-)}\) in the observational band.

### H. Testable predictions (high level)

- Algebraic real-space correlation with oscillatory ripples:
  \(\xi_\Theta(r)\sim r^{-(3-2\beta)}\cos(\kappa_\star r+\varphi_\star)\).
- Oscillatory structure in \(C_\ell\) with spacing \(\Delta\ell \sim \pi r_\star \kappa_\star\); the envelope slope around the first few peaks relates to \(\beta\).
- Robustness to masking/windows: changes \(W(r)\) modulate \(\Delta_\ell(k)\) but leave the intrinsic \(\kappa_\star\) spacing stable.
- Gravitational-wave sector (phase tensor tail): amplitude law \(|h|\propto (aM_\ast)^{-1}\) in the phase-FRW limit is consistent with the derivative-only construction (WAVE-1), no potentials required.
